PNWU Student Satisfaction Survey - Open Text Comments

These are real anonymized student comments from the 2023 and 2025 surveys.
The AI bot can use these to answer questions about qualitative student feedback.

=== 2023 COMMENTS (Question 21) ===

POSITIVE:
- "All of my interactions with library staff have been warm, professional, and generally excellent."
- "The librarians at this school are really great. Every time I've needed help faxing something, finding a book, or with research, they've gone above and beyond."
- "Megan, Mary and Jan deserve raises."
- "Very helpful and knowledgeable staff, no complaints!"
- "Library staff is AMAZING!!!"
- "Great and friendly staff."
- "Library staff has always been very helpful in responding to requests and instructive."

CONCERNS:
- "It is extremely frustrating to try to get help as a 3rd year student. When we're on rotations, we can't meet during normal business hours because we're at work and that is the only time you can book a librarian."
- "Never once used the library as a resource, not sure what their utility is in our first two years."
- "I wish we could have been oriented at the beginning of school to the library. I learned how to check out a book about 1 week ago."
- "Our library is very small. Libraries are often common study spaces but there really isn't much study space available."
- "I honestly didn't even know we had a library."

=== 2025 COMMENTS (Question 24) ===

POSITIVE:
- "The librarians are essential and super helpful with helping with research and getting resources to students."
- "The library staff have been incredible in providing support and guidance for independent research projects."
- "Mary and Jan are extraordinary. They have been such wonderful supports and guides throughout my time at PNWU."
- "Jan's the best."
- "Amazing, effective, and responsive staff, from the leadership on down."
- "The library is incredible. I use so many textbooks, databases, everything. Truly, they are the BEST. I owe my academic success to all they offer us."
- "The wonderful folks working at the library are a wonderful resource. The quickest I've ever received an ILL was two hours after request. They deserve a raise!!!!!"
- "I access information through the library databases every day during rotations."

CONCERNS:
- "I didn't even know we had a library."
- "Frequently had difficulty accessing specific OT journals for research."
- "I wish we had access to more databases, even commonly used ones like Elsevier or Wiley Online."
- "Access to further journals and resources for research would be extremely helpful, now we are very limited."
- "I run into the need for ILL a lot — if I'm reviewing something after rotations and only have one night, it's not practical to expect an ILL to come back in time."
- "I wish the library were bigger, with good seating for studying."

KEY THEMES ACROSS BOTH YEARS:
1. Staff are consistently praised — warmth, responsiveness, and expertise are recurring themes.
2. Space is a persistent concern — the library is seen as too small for studying.
3. Visibility gap — multiple students across both years said they did not know the library existed or what it offered.
4. Rotation students face access barriers — business hours don't work for clinical students.
5. Database and journal access gaps — students want more, especially clinical tools like AMBOSS, UWorld, Elsevier.
6. ILL is valued but speed matters for clinical students who need articles same-day.
